The Company

Greenfield Industrial Staffing has been placing skilled manufacturing workers across the Midwest since 2008. With 3,100 active workers spread across four plants — two automotive (Tier 1 suppliers to GM and Stellantis), one aerospace components manufacturer, and one FDA-regulated food processing facility — Greenfield operates in one of the most compliance-intensive corners of the staffing industry.

Every worker must maintain current certifications: OSHA 10 or OSHA 30 (depending on role), forklift operator licenses, clean room clearances for the aerospace plant, food handler certifications for the food-grade facility, and up-to-date drug testing. A single expired certification can trigger an OSHA fine, a client audit failure, or — worst case — a safety incident.

The Challenge

In 2024, Greenfield's compliance record fell apart:

3 compliance violations in 12 months: Two workers placed at the aerospace plant without current OSHA 30 certifications. One worker assigned to the food processing facility with an expired food handler card. Total fines: $180,000. Total client trust lost: immeasurable.

  • Certification data was tracked in a combination of Excel spreadsheets, Bullhorn notes fields, and a coordinator's memory. No single source of truth.
  • A near-miss safety incident: a worker without current forklift certification was placed on a powered industrial truck at the GM supplier. No injury, but the client issued a formal safety stand-down and threatened contract termination.
  • Fill time averaged 45 minutes because coordinators had to manually cross-reference certification spreadsheets before making each call. For urgent call-outs, coordinators sometimes skipped the check — which is exactly how the violations happened.
  • The compliance director spent 20 hours per week auditing placement records retroactively — catching problems after the fact instead of preventing them.

"The violations weren't happening because our coordinators didn't care," says Robert Tanaka, Compliance Director. "They were happening because we'd built a system where doing the right thing — checking every certification before every placement — took so long that people cut corners under pressure. You can't blame the people when the process is the problem."

The Solution

Greenfield deployed MyHR with a specific focus on the eligibility engine — the component that determines which workers can be contacted for which shifts:

  • Certification sync: All certification data was imported from Bullhorn and the spreadsheets into MyHR's structured database. Each certification type was mapped to specific sites and roles with hard expiration enforcement.
  • Automatic exclusion: Workers with any expired or missing required certification are automatically excluded from outreach for affected shifts. There is no override without compliance director approval — and every override is logged.
  • Proactive expiration alerts: Workers receive automated reminders 30, 14, and 7 days before any certification expires, with instructions for renewal. Coordinators receive alerts too, so they can help workers schedule renewals.
  • Drug test currency: Drug test results (pass/fail only — MyHR doesn't store detailed medical data) are tracked with facility-specific validity periods. Workers whose tests are expiring are flagged before they become ineligible.

The Results

Nine months after deployment:

  • Compliance violations: 3 in 2024 → 0 in 9 months of MyHR operation
  • Fill time: 45 min → 6 min (certification checks that took 8-12 minutes per worker happen instantly)
  • Penalty savings: $430K in avoided fines (extrapolated from 2024 violation rate)
  • Compliance director audit time: 20 hours/week → 4 hours/week (most auditing is now automated)
  • Certification renewal rate: 78% → 96% (proactive reminders dramatically reduced expirations)
  • Client audit results: Passed all four quarterly client audits with zero findings for the first time in company history

"For the first time in my career, I can guarantee — not hope, guarantee — that every worker on every shift has every required certification. The system won't let it happen any other way. That near-miss at the GM plant haunted me for months. It can't happen now. I sleep better."

— Robert Tanaka, Compliance Director, Greenfield Industrial Staffing

The Client Impact

Greenfield's four plant clients all renewed their contracts in 2025-2026, with two expanding coverage to additional shifts. The aerospace manufacturer — the most compliance-sensitive client — specifically cited Greenfield's "transformed compliance infrastructure" in their vendor evaluation. The GM Tier 1 supplier upgraded Greenfield from "conditional" to "preferred" vendor status, unlocking access to additional plant locations.
