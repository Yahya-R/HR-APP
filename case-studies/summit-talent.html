The Company

Summit Talent Partners is a boutique hospitality staffing firm operating in the New York City and northern New Jersey metro area. Founded in 2016 by Natalie Cho, a former event director at a major hotel group, Summit places 1,800 workers in hotels, banquet halls, convention centers, and corporate event venues. They handle staffing for over 300 events per month — from 15-person board dinners to 3,000-person trade shows.

The Challenge

Hospitality staffing is fundamentally different from industrial or warehouse staffing in one critical way: there's no margin for error on timing. When a banquet server doesn't show up two hours before a 500-person gala, there is no "we'll get someone there by lunch." The event starts when it starts.

By early 2025, Summit's model was cracking under this pressure:

  • Client retention dropped from 88% to 71% in 18 months — event planners were switching to competitors after repeated coverage failures
  • Average response time: 22 minutes for a cancellation — unacceptable when the event starts in 2 hours
  • Lost the Javits Center contract ($380K/year), their highest-profile account, after three consecutive events with no-shows. The convention center's events director told Natalie directly: "We can't risk it anymore."
  • Workers in the hospitality pool were especially hard to reach by phone — many worked second jobs and had inconsistent availability

"Losing Javits was the wake-up call," says Natalie. "That contract wasn't just revenue — it was our reputation. Every event planner in New York knows the Javits Center. When they dropped us, the whisper network lit up. We had three other clients ask us about it within a week."

The Solution

Summit implemented MyHR with a custom urgency tier system designed specifically for event staffing:

  • Red Alert (event in <4 hours): Simultaneous calls + texts to every eligible worker in the pool. Voice agent uses an urgent but professional tone. Workers who accept are immediately confirmed with venue address and arrival time.
  • Orange (event in 4-24 hours): Text-first outreach to the top 30 eligible workers based on proximity and past reliability. Voice calls escalate after 10 minutes if not enough responses.
  • Standard (event in >24 hours): Text-only outreach with 2-hour response window. Optimized for worker convenience.

The urgency tiers were key — in hospitality, a cancellation 3 days before an event is a completely different problem than a cancellation 3 hours before. MyHR treats them differently.

The Results

After 90 days:

  • Client retention: 71% → 94%
  • Average response time: 22 min → 3.2 min (Red Alert: 2.1 min)
  • No-show rate: 14% → 3.8%
  • Revenue recovered from previously unfilled positions: $840K annually
  • Worker satisfaction: NPS for "communication experience" went from 31 to 67

But the headline result was winning back the Javits Center.

Winning Back Javits

Three months after deploying MyHR, Natalie reached back out to the Javits Center events director and proposed a 90-day trial at a reduced rate. "I told her, 'We fixed the problem. Give us 90 days and judge us on the data.'"

During the trial, Summit covered 47 events at the Javits Center. Fill rate: 98.2%. Average response time for day-of cancellations: 2.4 minutes. Zero events with coverage gaps.

The Javits Center renewed the full contract — with a 12% rate increase.

"When the Javits Center called to renew, I cried. Not because of the revenue — because it meant we'd actually fixed the thing that was broken. Our workers are happier, our clients trust us again, and my team isn't drowning in phone calls. That's what I started this company for."

— Natalie Cho, Founder & CEO, Summit Talent Partners
