Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2026 · Last Updated: January 1, 2026

MyHR Technologies, Inc. ("MyHR," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of our customers, their workers, and visitors to our website. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information in connection with our platform, services, and website (collectively, the "Service").

1. Information We Collect

Information Provided by Customers

When staffing agencies use MyHR, they provide us with worker information necessary to deliver the Service. This includes:

  • Worker contact information: Names, phone numbers, email addresses
  • Employment data: Job titles, skills, certifications (e.g., OSHA 10/30, forklift certification), pay rates, employment status
  • Availability data: Shift preferences, scheduling constraints, time-off records
  • Compliance data: Drug test results (pass/fail status only), background check clearance status, work authorization status
  • Location data: Worker zip code or city (used for proximity-based shift matching)

Information Generated Through the Service

  • Communication logs: Records of calls, texts, and messages sent through the platform, including timestamps, duration, and content
  • Voice recordings: Audio recordings of AI voice agent conversations with workers (where legally permitted and with proper consent)
  • Response data: Worker responses to shift offers (accepted, declined, no response), response times, and stated reasons for declination
  • Behavioral patterns: Aggregated patterns of shift acceptance, preferred work schedules, and communication channel preferences

Information from Website Visitors

  • Contact form submissions: Name, email, company, phone number, message content
  • Usage data: Pages visited, time on site, referral source, browser type, device type, IP address
  • Cookie data: See Section 8 (Cookies) below

3. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal information to third parties. We share personal information only in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers: We use third-party vendors to help deliver the Service, including cloud hosting (AWS), telephony (Twilio, Telnyx), speech-to-text processing, and analytics. These providers are contractually bound to use personal information only as necessary to provide their services to us.
  • Customer access: Worker Data is accessible to the staffing agency (Customer) that provided it. Workers may contact us to understand how their data is being used.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose personal information when required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, personal information may be transferred to the successor entity. We will notify affected parties of any such transfer.

4. Data Security

We maintain a comprehensive security program designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our security measures include:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
  • SOC 2 Type II certification, audited annually by an independent third-party firm
  • Role-based access controls with multi-factor authentication for all internal systems
  • Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments
  • 24/7 security monitoring and incident response procedures
  • Employee security training and background checks
  • Data center security through our hosting provider (AWS), which maintains ISO 27001, SOC 1/2/3, and FedRAMP certifications

5. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide the Service and fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

  • Active account data: Retained for the duration of the customer relationship plus 90 days
  • Voice recordings: Retained for 90 days unless the Customer configures a shorter retention period. Customers can request immediate deletion at any time.
  • Communication logs: Retained for 12 months for compliance and audit purposes
  • Anonymized analytics: Retained indefinitely (cannot be used to identify individuals)
  • Website visitor data: Retained for 24 months

6. Your Rights

California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect, use, and disclose about you
  • Request deletion of your personal information
  • Opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information (note: we do not sell personal information)
  • Request correction of inaccurate personal information
  • Limit the use of sensitive personal information
  • Not be discriminated against for exercising your privacy rights

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@myhr.ai or call 1-888-555-MYHR. We will respond to verified requests within 45 days.

European Residents (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict processing, data portability, and object to processing of your personal data. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. Contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@myhr.ai.

All Users

Regardless of your location, you may:

  • Request a copy of personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Opt out of marketing communications at any time
  • Request deletion of your account and associated data

7. Automated Decision-Making

MyHR uses automated systems to determine which workers are eligible for specific shifts based on criteria set by the Customer (certifications, availability, location, etc.) and to prioritize outreach order. These automated decisions are based on objective criteria configured by the Customer and are subject to human oversight. Workers or Customers may request human review of any automated decision by contacting support@myhr.ai.

8. Cookies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Essential cookies: Enable core website functionality (session management, security). Cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website (Google Analytics). Can be disabled via cookie preferences.
  • Marketing cookies: Used to deliver relevant advertising to website visitors. Can be disabled via cookie preferences. Not used within the platform itself.

You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings or through the cookie consent banner displayed on our website.

9. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child under 18, we will take steps to delete such information promptly.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or other factors. We will notify Customers of material changes via email at least 30 days before they take effect. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page indicates when this Privacy Policy was last revised.
